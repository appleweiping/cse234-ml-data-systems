Discussion for 2.1 — myAllreduce and myAlltoall vs. the MPI built-ins

myAllreduce (reduce-to-root + broadcast, tree depth = 2 hops effectively O(n)
at the root): measured on 8 ranks, 100 runs, MS-MPI on a single 16-logical-core
Windows box: MPI.Allreduce ~0.000050 s vs myAllreduce ~0.000120 s (~2.4x slower).

myAlltoall (pairwise Sendrecv over all peers): MPI.Alltoall ~0.0079 s vs
myAlltoall ~0.0158 s (~2x slower).

Why the gap: the built-ins use bandwidth-optimal, log-depth algorithms
(recursive-halving / recursive-doubling, ring, Bruck) and can overlap
communication, whereas our versions are linear. myAllreduce funnels all data
through rank 0, so the root does O(n) sequential receives and sends and becomes
a serialization bottleneck; a real all-reduce spreads that work with a
reduce-scatter followed by an all-gather in O(log n) rounds. myAlltoall performs
n-1 blocking Sendrecv exchanges per rank with no pipelining, while MPI schedules
the exchanges to keep the network busy and avoid congestion. Both of ours are
correct (100/100 runs match the built-in) but pay for their simplicity in
latency. Note all runs are shared-memory loopback on one node, so absolute
numbers are dominated by copy/launch overhead rather than real network cost.
